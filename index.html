What's in A Footprint?

The general understanding of carbon footprint infers a demand centered on individualism: that people rethink their personal lifestyles, daily routines, and individual choices. What’s in a Footprint? aims to revisit the concept of carbon footprint and invite citizens to critically evaluate how those ‘actions’ relate to communities, to global systems, and to overarching ideologies.

AR Scavenger Hunt @ AMNH

In celebration of Earth Day 2024, the American Museum of Natural History, collaborating with Columbia University’s Brown Institute, Digital Futures Institute, and Learning the Earth with Artificial Intelligence and Physics (LEAP), launched an AR Scavenger Hunt to educate visitors about carbon cycle. Built upon six dioramas of Milstein Hall of Ocean Life, the game is location-based to immerse users in the physical environment of the museum to find the story of sea animals' contributions in carbon capture under the sea.
